Explore Tarlac

ECO-TOURISM

Nestled in the heart of Central Luzon, Tarlac Province is a hidden gem for eco-tourism enthusiasts, offering a blend of lush landscapes and rich biodiversity. With its rolling hills, vibrant rice fields, and serene lakes, Tarlac invites travelers to immerse themselves in nature's tranquility.

Jose V. Yap Sports and Recreational Complex

San Jose, Tarlac

Jose V. Yap Sports and Recreational Complex - former Tarlac Recreational Park is named in honor of Jose V. Yap, a visionary leader and advocate for sports and community development, this state-of-the-art complex was built to foster physical well-being and promote unity. Opened to the public as a hub for athletes and families alike, it has since become a symbol of progress and excellence, offering a space for both competitive sports and recreational activities.


At the Jose V Yap Sports and Recreational Complex, there’s never a dull moment! Dive into action with a variety of sports—play basketball on the courts, hit the track for a jog, or serve up some fun with tennis, golf, and ATV. Families can enjoy peaceful picnics in green spaces or take a leisurely stroll around the scenic grounds. Whether you’re looking to compete, relax, or simply stay active, this is the place to be for fun, fitness, and endless possibilities!

Where passion meets play – the Jose V Yap Sports and Recreational Complex is your ultimate destination for fitness, fun, and friendly competition.

Capas National Shrine

Barangay Aranguren, Capas, Tarlac

Standing as a solemn tribute to the bravery and sacrifice of Filipino and American soldiers during World War II, the Capas National Shrine commemorates the harrowing history of the Death March. Located in Capas, Tarlac, it honors those who endured unimaginable hardship and those who perished along the march. The shrine features a majestic monument, as well as peaceful grounds where visitors can reflect on the courage and resilience of the fallen heroes. The site serves as a poignant reminder of the sacrifices made for freedom and peace.


In its center, there is a three-pronged pillar terminating into a sharpened peak. A seventy-meter concrete obelisk soaring above the trees and pointing to the great blue sky.


The shrine also features beautifully landscaped gardens, walking paths, and informative displays about the historical events of the Bataan Death March. Notably, the site includes a museum where artifacts and exhibits help tell the story of the courageous soldiers who endured the march. The peaceful atmosphere of the grounds invites visitors to pay their respects while learning about one of the darkest yet most important chapters in Philippine history."

It is more than just a memorial — it's a place of reflection and remembrance. The Shrine is raw. It is memorial to our fallen heroes. A place for their families and kin to touch their names on that black marble wall and remember.

Barangay Maasin, San Clemente, Tarlac

At Canding Falls, adventure meets serenity. You can take a dip in the cool, clear waters at the base of the falls or enjoy a refreshing shower as the water cascades down from the rocks above. For those feeling more adventurous, the short trek to the falls offers a rewarding experience, as you pass through lush forests, listen to the sounds of nature, and take in breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. The falls' surroundings are dotted with vibrant greenery, offering perfect spots for picnics, photography, or simply unwinding by the water. If you’re looking to connect with nature, this hidden paradise gives you a chance to relax, swim, explore, and enjoy the peaceful, scenic beauty of the area.

Monasterio De Tarlac

San Jose, Tarlac, Philippines

The Monasterio de Tarlac is a Benedictine monastery that was established in 2004. It serves as a peaceful spiritual retreat and pilgrimage site, notably housing a Relic of the True Cross, a fragment believed to be from the cross of Jesus Christ. The monastery's simple yet striking architecture blends with its natural surroundings, offering visitors a place for reflection and faith. It stands as a symbol of the Philippines' Catholic heritage and devotion.

At the Monasterio de Tarlac, visitors can enjoy a peaceful blend of spiritual reflection and natural beauty. One of the main highlights is the Relic of the True Cross, which pilgrims can venerate, adding a deeply spiritual dimension to their visit. The monastery offers a serene environment for prayer, meditation, and quiet contemplation, allowing guests to reconnect with their faith. It provides stunning panoramic views of Tarlac’s lush landscape, with designated viewpoints perfect for photography or simply taking in the tranquility of the area.

Sitio tala, also known as "Little Baguio" is a scenic destination renowned for its cool climate and lush, mountainous landscape. Lubigan Picnic Ground

Lubigan, San Jose, Tarlac, Philippines

Dueg-Kapya Ken Talna, also known as Dueg-Kapya, is renowned for its serene landscape, featuring a combination of lush green hills, clear waters, and amazing views. The name "Dueg-Kapya Ken Talna" translates to "cool water" in the local Ilocano dialect, reflecting the refreshing ambiance you will feel when you’re in the area.

The area is equipped with nipa huts available for rent, offering comfortable spaces to unwind and enjoy meals amidst the lush landscape. For those who prefer a more rustic experience, bringing a blanket or banig to set up a personal picnic spot is also a popular choice. The riverbank features both rocky and muddy sections, adding to the natural charm of the environment.


One of the highlights of the Lubigan Picnic Ground is its proximity to Mt. Ngile, which provides a stunning backdrop and contributes to the area's cool and refreshing atmosphere. The site is easily accessible, located just off the main road, making it a convenient destination for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

Mt. Kawayan

Brgy. Lubigan, San Jose, Tarlac

Mount Kawayan, located in Brgy. Lubigan, San Jose, Tarlac, is a relatively lesser-known but captivating mountain in Central Luzon. Standing at approximately 1,045 meters above sea level, it offers a moderately challenging trek suitable for both novice and experienced hikers. The mountain is named after the local kawayan (bamboo) trees that thrive in the area, adding to the natural charm of the hike.

The trail takes you through lush forests, diverse flora, and a mix of steep and gradual slopes. Hikers are rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes, including nearby mountains and expansive plains. The summit offers a scenic vantage point, perfect for photo opportunities and a peaceful retreat in nature. Mount Kawayan is also home to various wildlife, making it a great spot for nature enthusiasts. The mountain's serene atmosphere and beautiful surroundings make it an ideal destination for those seeking a quiet and rewarding outdoor experience.

Mt. Bungkol-Baka

Brgy. Iba, San Jose, Tarlac

Mt. Bungkol Baka, standing at 617 meters above sea level, is the highest peak in San Jose, Tarlac. It is part of the San Jose Circuit, a 17.7-kilometer hiking trail that also includes Mt. Tangisan (491m) and Mt. Kawayan (595m). The trail begins at Sitio San Pedro, crossing a river before ascending through banana plantations and streams. The hike is moderately challenging, with steep sections and exposure to the sun, but offers rewarding panoramic views of Central Luzon's plains and the Zambales mountain range.

Hikers typically start with Mt. Tangisan, proceed to Mt. Bungkol Baka, and finish with Mt. Kawayan, completing the circuit within a day or as an overnight trek. The summit of Mt. Bungkol Baka provides breathtaking scenery, making it a highlight of the trail. Its diverse terrain and scenic landscapes make it a must-visit destination for adventure seekers looking for a rewarding hiking experience in Tarlac.

Mt. Tangisan

Brgy. Iba, San Jose, Tarlac

Mt. Tangisan, rising 491 meters above sea level in San Jose, Tarlac, is part of the San Jose Circuit, a 17.7-kilometer trail that also includes Mt. Bungkol Baka and Mt. Kawayan. The hike starts at Sitio San Pedro, Barangay Iba, requiring a river crossing before ascending through diverse terrains. The trail offers breathtaking views of Mt. Arayat and the Zambales Mountain Range, making it a rewarding adventure for hikers. Along the way, trekkers can experience lush flora, fauna, and scenic spots like Pangasaan Lake and the Meriones River.

A moderately challenging trek, Mt. Tangisan is ideal for hikers with a reasonable fitness level, typically taking around 10 hours to complete the full circuit. The trail features steep inclines, gentle slopes, and forested paths, providing a mix of adventure and relaxation. Hikers can immerse themselves in nature, enjoy panoramic landscapes, and take in the peaceful surroundings. Whether seeking a physical challenge or simply appreciating Tarlac’s natural beauty, Mt. Tangisan offers an enjoyable and fulfilling outdoor experience.

Mt. Damas

Sitio Dueg, Brgy. Maasin, San Clemente

Mt. Damas, standing at approximately 685 meters above sea level, is located near the borders of Mayantoc, Camiling, and San Clemente in Tarlac, Philippines. Despite its moderate elevation, it presents a challenging climb due to its steep ascents, river crossings, and technical trails. The journey to the summit typically begins at the Dueg Resettlement area in San Clemente, a community established after the Mt. Pinatubo eruption.

Due to its technical trails, Mt. Damas is recommended for experienced hikers who are physically prepared and equipped with proper gear. Hiring a local guide is advised for safety. Those who take on the challenge are rewarded with breathtaking views, rugged landscapes, and a thrilling adventure in nature.

Mt. Pinatubo Trek

Brgy. Santa Juliana, Capas, Tarlac

Rising from history and nature’s raw power, Mount Pinatubo is an iconic destination that offers an unforgettable adventure. Known for its legendary 1991 eruption, this once-devastating volcano has transformed into a breathtaking wonder, featuring a stunning turquoise crater lake surrounded by dramatic cliffs and rugged landscapes.

Hiking to Mount Pinatubo is an experience like no other. The trek takes you through lahar-carved canyons, scenic trails, and unique rock formations, leading to the awe-inspiring crater lake—an ideal spot for sightseeing and photography. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or a nature lover, this journey promises both thrill and beauty. Embark on the ultimate adventure and witness the majesty of Mount Pinatubo!
