Explore Tarlac

ADVENTURE

Tarlac is a great place for fun and adventure in the Philippines. Hike Mount Pinatubo to see its beautiful crater lake, ride an ATV along the Sacobia River, or try the zipline at Tarlac Recreational Park. Visit the peaceful Monasterio de Tarlac for amazing views. Tarlac is perfect for nature lovers and thrill-seekers!

Para Motor

Bamban, Tarlac

Paramotoring in Tarlac began as a recreational activity for local flying enthusiasts and has grown into a popular attraction for tourists seeking adventure. The province’s wide-open spaces, favorable weather, and scenic views make it an ideal spot for this sport. Over the years, it has become a symbol of Tarlac’s commitment to promoting eco-friendly tourism and outdoor adventure.

Experience the thrill of flying with paramotoring in Tarlac! Soar above stunning landscapes, with breathtaking views of mountains, rivers, and vast fields. Feel the freedom of the open sky as you glide smoothly, making it perfect for adventure seekers and first-time flyers. With professional guides ensuring safety, it’s an unforgettable experience offering excitement, relaxation, and incredible photo opportunities. Don’t miss the chance to see Tarlac from a whole new perspective!


: Provincial Government of Tarlac

Atv

Sta. Juliana, Capas, Tarlac

Explore the rugged terrains of Tarlac with an exciting ATV ride! Traverse muddy trails, rocky paths, and river crossings while enjoying the province’s stunning natural scenery. Perfect for thrill-seekers and nature lovers, the ATV experience offers an adrenaline rush and breathtaking views of mountains, rivers, and lush landscapes. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced rider, it’s a fun and unforgettable adventure.

ATV riding in Tarlac began as a way to explore the areas surrounding Mount Pinatubo after its 1991 eruption. The challenging trails created by lahar flows and volcanic ash deposits soon attracted adventure enthusiasts. Over the years, ATV tours became a popular tourist activity, combining thrilling rides with scenic views and a glimpse into the province’s natural and cultural heritage.


: Exploring the Lahar Trail of Mt. Pinatubo with Capas ATV Adventure (Website)

Kayak

San Jose, Tarlac

Kayaking at Tarlac Recreational Park offers a fun and relaxing way to explore calm waters surrounded by lush greenery. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced paddler, the park’s peaceful lake provides the perfect setting for a leisurely ride or a friendly race with family and friends. It’s a great activity to enjoy nature while getting a light workout.

Tarlac Recreational Park, established in 2009, was developed to promote sports, leisure, and eco-tourism in the province. Originally designed as a training venue for athletes, the park expanded its offerings to include activities like kayaking, fishing, and camping. Today, it’s a popular destination for both locals and tourists seeking outdoor adventures and relaxation.


: Provincial Government of Tarlac

Hiking

Capas, Tarlac (north side)
Camiling, Tarlac

Tarlac is a great destination for hiking, offering trails that lead to stunning natural wonders. Adventurers can trek to Mount Pinatubo’s crater lake, known for its breathtaking views and unique landscape formed by the 1991 volcanic eruption. Other trails, like Mount Damas, challenge hikers with steep paths and reward them with waterfalls and panoramic scenery. With its mix of easy and challenging routes, Tarlac is perfect for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking in Tarlac has grown in popularity since the early 2000s, especially after Mount Pinatubo became a famous trekking spot. The province’s mountainous areas, once known mainly to locals, have been developed into eco-tourism sites to attract more visitors. Today, hiking is not only a recreational activity but also a way for Tarlac to promote environmental awareness and sustainable tourism.

: Mt. Damas Traverse Dayhike Circuit, San Clemente, Tarlac (Website)

Mountain Bike

San Jose, Tarlac

Mountain biking at Monasterio de Tarlac offers an exciting adventure through scenic trails surrounded by lush forests, rolling hills, and breathtaking views. Riders can enjoy a mix of challenging terrains and peaceful paths leading to the iconic 30-foot Christ statue overlooking the province. It’s a perfect spot for both adrenaline seekers and those looking to appreciate nature’s beauty.

The Monasterio de Tarlac, established in 2001, is part of the Mount Resurrection Eco Park in San Jose, Tarlac. Originally built as a place for prayer and reflection, it has become a popular destination for both pilgrims and outdoor enthusiasts. Over time, its surrounding landscapes attracted mountain bikers, turning the area into a favored spot for eco-tourism and adventure sports.


: Provincial Government of Tarlac
